SunTzu

A Strategic Reasoning Benchmark for Large Language Models

Paper Code Demo

We introduce SunTzu, a novel benchmark for evaluating LLMs in long-horizon decision-making tasks, adapted from StarCraft II. We also establish a hierarchical baseline algorithm that achieves self-improvement through supervised fine-tuning.

Abstract

Recent advancements in decision-making algorithms, such as reinforcement learning (RL), have achieved significant breakthroughs, particularly in mastering precise control. However, these methods struggle with long-horizon decision-making tasks, which require strategic reasoning to break down into sub-tasks effectively. Conversely, Large Language Models (LLMs), trained on vast corpora, have internalized high-dimensional abstractions of world knowledge, causal relationships, and logical laws, endowing them with powerful reasoning capabilities.

To bridge the gap between traditional decision-making algorithms and high-level strategic reasoning, we introduce SunTzu, a novel benchmark adapted for LLMs within the complex, long-horizon environment of StarCraft II. Furthermore, we establish a baseline algorithm on this benchmark and evaluate state-of-the-art open-source models. Our work aims to inspire researchers to endow current algorithms with strategic reasoning capabilities, thereby advancing the field of intelligent decision-making.

SunTzu: A Strategic Reasoning Benchmark

StarCraft II features a vast space of cyclic, non-transitive strategies and counter-strategies. This strategic depth arises because each race offers a distinct playstyle and unique mechanics. To bridge the gap between traditional decision-making algorithms and high-level strategic reasoning, we introduce SunTzu, a benchmark designed to evaluate LLMs in complex, long-horizon tasks.

Comparison of SunTzu with prior benchmarks, highlighting its support for full game context, all races, and complete action spaces.


Optimized Observations for LLMs

LLMs struggle with raw game data due to challenges in spatial reasoning from text and information overload from numerous units. SunTzu introduces two key techniques to format game states into LLM-friendly text:

  • Proximity-based Unit Ordering: Units are listed based on their spatial closeness, helping the LLM infer relationships and identify interactable structures (e.g., a Barracks with an attached Tech Lab).
  • Unit Aggregation: Routine units like workers are grouped into a single summary, reducing redundant information and allowing the agent to focus on high-level strategy.

Illustration of SunTzu's text-based interface and observation optimization techniques.

Hierarchical Baseline Algorithm

We propose a hierarchical baseline algorithm designed to adapt LLMs to long-horizon decision-making tasks within SunTzu. It employs a hierarchical architecture that decouples strategic planning from tactical execution, enabling continuous self-improvement through supervised fine-tuning.

The hierarchical baseline framework, showing the Planner-Executor-Verifier structure for self-correction and the SFT loop for self-improvement.


Hierarchical Self-Correction Framework

To address the vast action space and strict syntax requirements, our baseline uses a two-tier architecture:

  • Planner: Generates high-level strategic commands in natural language (e.g., "Build a Barracks") based on observations.
  • Executor: Translates the Planner's natural language commands into executable, low-level JSON actions.

Both modules incorporate Verifier components that facilitate iterative self-correction. If the Planner's output violates environmental constraints, its Verifier provides structured feedback for refinement. The Executor's Verifier validates JSON syntax, parameter correctness, and action feasibility, enabling the agent to iteratively correct decisions before execution.

Self-Improvement via Fine-Tuning

Our baseline establishes a self-improvement loop by collecting gameplay data from winning trajectories. We design a scoring function to identify high-quality decisions that yield short-term rewards and contribute to long-term strategic advantages. These data points are used to fine-tune the Planner, Executor, and Verifier through:

  • Verifier-guided rejection sampling: Training on verified high-quality outputs while masking intermediate refinement steps.
  • Implicit contrastive learning: Training the Verifier on accepted vs. rejected command pairs to capture boundaries between valid and invalid decisions.

Results

Our experiments demonstrate that SunTzu effectively evaluates LLMs in long-horizon decision-making tasks through Elo ratings and comprehensive metrics. StarEvolve achieves superior performance compared to directly applying LLMs to this environment, with the SFT-trained version reaching the highest Elo ratings across all races.
